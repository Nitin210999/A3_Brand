2024 Acura RDX vs 2024 Infiniti QX50

The compact luxury crossover segment is a battlefield, where titans clash for the title of best sport crossover. In 2024, two contenders rise: the ever-popular 2024 Acura RDX and the 2024 Infiniti QX50. Both boast sleek designs, powerful engines, and a nice cabin experience. But which one truly reigns supreme?

This head-to-head comparison dives deep into the strengths and weaknesses of these two automotive warriors. We'll look at their performance, features, technology, and safety measures, in our quest to crown the champion of the compact luxury crossover arena!

Power and Performance

While both the 2024 Infiniti QX50 and 2024 Acura RDX offer compelling options in the compact luxury crossover segment, the RDX emerges as the clear winner for those seeking the best sport crossover experience.

Both vehicles boast turbocharged 2.0-liter four cylinder engines, but the Acura RDX 2024 edges out the QX50 with more horsepower (272 HP compared to 268 HP) and a matching 280 lb.-ft. of torque. This translates to a more exhilarating driving feel, especially noticeable during acceleration and highway merges. Additionally, the RDX's max towing capacity of 1,500 lbs. gives it added versatility for weekend adventures with bikes or small trailers.

Acura's commitment to driving exhilaration shines through in the RDX's superior handling. Its retuned Adaptive Damper system and adjustable Integrated Dynamics System (IDS) offer four distinct driving modes – Comfort, Normal, Snow, and Sport – allowing you to tailor the driving experience to your preferences. Sport mode sharpens reflexes, enhancing throttle response, steering feel, and damper stiffness for a truly exhilarating drive.

The Acura RDX 2024’s dual-pinion, variable-ratio electric power steering delivers sharp and responsive handling, adapting to your desired level of effort based on the chosen driving mode. Comfort mode provides effortless maneuvering, while Sport mode firms up the steering for a more connected and engaging feel. The upgraded iBooster braking system further inspires confidence with improved stopping power and a firmer pedal feel, ensuring precise control behind the wheel.

Acura goes beyond pure horsepower to elevate the driving experience. Features like paddle shifters for manual gear changes and standard Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) add to the RDX's sporty character. Meanwhile, the QX50's continuously variable transmission (CVT) can feel less engaging than the RDX's 10-speed automatic transmission, particularly for enthusiasts seeking a dynamic driving experience. .

While both the 2024 Infiniti QX50 and 2024 Acura RDX are compelling choices, the RDX's sharper powertrain, superior handling, exceptional build quality, and driver-focused features solidify its position as the true contender for the title of best sport crossover. For those seeking an exhilarating and dynamic driving experience in a luxurious and versatile package, the Acura RDX stands out as the clear choice.

Overall Design

The design of the 2024 Acura RDX effortlessly outshines the 2024 Infiniti QX50, making the Acura RDX the epitome of the best sport crossover.

Starting with the interior, the Acura RDX offers a more luxurious and comfortable experience with Front 12-Way Power Seats, including Power Lumbar Support, heated front seats, and a leather-wrapped steering wheel. In contrast, the Infiniti QX50 falls short with an 8-way power front seat, lacking the comprehensive adjustments and premium materials found in the Acura RDX.

Cargo space is another area where the Acura RDX excels, providing up to 79.8 cu.-ft. of max cargo volume when the 60/40-split rear seats are folded down. This surpasses the Infiniti QX50's limited 65.1 cubic feet of cargo space. The intelligently engineered rear cargo underfloor storage in the Acura RDX adds an extra layer of versatility, allowing users to adapt to the demands of their journey.

Aesthetic elements play a crucial role in the overall design, and the Acura RDX 2024 proves to be superior in this aspect as well. The standard panoramic moonroof in the Acura RDX enhances the sense of openness and spaciousness inside the cabin, providing a feature that is optional in the Infiniti QX50.

The Acura RDX's cabin is a testament to its attention to detail, featuring premium materials such as brushed aluminum, open-pore wood accents, and full-grain Premium Milano leather seating, creating a bespoke and serene environment.

The exterior design of the 2024 Acura RDX further solidifies its status as the best sport crossover. With aggressive body lines emanating from the striking Diamond Pentagon grille, sculpted rear, LED-illuminated taillights, and rectangular dual exhaust finishers, the Acura RDX exudes a bold and premium presence on the road. The iconic Jewel Eye LED headlights not only provide superior down-road visibility but also contribute to the distinctive appearance of the Acura RDX, enhancing its overall allure.

To add a final touch, the Acura RDX 2024 offers premium paint options, including Performance Red Pearl, Liquid Carbon Metallic, Majestic Black Pearl, Platinum White Pearl, Phantom Violet Pearl, and Apex Blue Pearl. These high-quality pigments, applied in a series of tinted clear coats, result in a brilliant chromatic color with a deep, rich finish, elevating the Acura RDX's exterior aesthetics.

The 2024 Acura RDX outshines the 2024 Infiniti QX50 in design, providing a more luxurious interior, superior cargo space, advanced features, and an aggressive yet sophisticated exterior.

Tech Capabilities

When comparing the tech capabilities of the 2024 Acura RDX and the 2024 Infiniti QX50, it's evident that the Acura RDX stands out as the best sport crossover with more advanced features and a superior technological interface.

The Acura RDX boasts a 10.2-inch HD dual-content infotainment display, surpassing the Infiniti QX50's smaller 7-inch and 8-inch center displays. This larger, high-definition screen in the Acura RDX enhances the overall user experience, providing a more immersive and visually appealing interface.

One distinctive feature that sets the Acura RDX 2024 apart is the True Touchpad Interface. This technology utilizes absolute positioning, allowing users to easily control the 10.2-inch HD display. The touchpad corresponds directly to specific spots on the screen, ensuring a more intuitive and user-friendly interaction. This technology enhances safety by reducing distractions, keeping the driver focused on the road, a feature not available in the Infiniti QX50.

In terms of connectivity, both vehicles offer Wi-Fi hotspot capability, Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and a wireless charging pad. However, the Acura RDX goes a step further by incorporating Alexa built-in, providing additional voice-activated functionality that enhances the overall convenience and accessibility of the vehicle. This feature is not available in the Infiniti QX50, giving the Acura RDX a technological edge.

Another tech highlight of the Acura RDX 2024 is the available ELS Studio 3D Premium Audio System. Engineered by Grammy Award-winning recording producer Elliot Scheiner, this audio system boasts 16 speakers, including four in the headliner. The result is an immaculate and immersive sound experience that elevates the driving journey.

Safety Features

The 2024 Acura RDX takes safety to a new level with its comprehensive suite of advanced safety and driver-assistive technologies known as AcuraWatch. This suite is designed to enhance driver awareness and help avoid collisions, making the Acura RDX a leader in safety among the best sport crossovers. Notably, the 2024 Infiniti QX50 falls short as it lacks this comprehensive safety system, giving the Acura RDX 2024 an advantage.

AcuraWatch includes several key features that contribute to the overall safety of the Acura RDX. Some of them are below:

The Collision Mitigation Braking System (CMBS) is a standout feature, alerting drivers to potential frontal impacts and, if necessary, applying the brakes to avoid or reduce the collision impact. This proactive system goes beyond mere warnings, actively assisting the driver in mitigating the severity of collisions.

The Road Departure Mitigation (RDM) feature adds an extra layer of safety by alerting the driver to unintentional departures from a detected lane or roadway. In the event of such a departure, the system can apply steering torque and braking to help keep the vehicle safely on the road, preventing potential accidents caused by lane drift.

Another noteworthy safety feature in the Acura RDX is the Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) with Low-Speed Follow. This technology is designed to reduce driver fatigue by maintaining a set speed and distance from the vehicle detected ahead. The low-speed follow feature is particularly beneficial, as it can bring the vehicle to a complete stop if the detected vehicle slows down to a stop. This not only enhances convenience during traffic but also contributes to overall safety.

It's essential to highlight that, despite these advanced safety features, the driver remains responsible for safely operating the vehicle and avoiding collisions. Acura emphasizes the importance of driver engagement, acknowledging that technology should be a complement to responsible driving rather than a substitute.

Conclusion

In the grand showdown between the 2024 Acura RDX and the 2024 Infiniti QX50, the Acura RDX firmly asserts its dominance as the best sport crossover of the year. With its superior power, advanced tech capabilities, meticulously designed interior, and comprehensive safety features, the Acura RDX leaves an indelible mark on the landscape of premium sport crossovers.
